▲ Coordinates and direction as shown using the F3 key (XYZ and positive X)

① Coordinates (X / Y / Z) 【Importance: ★★★★★】

  • Shows your current location in the world.
  • The first number is the X-coordinate (horizontal), and the third is the Z-coordinate (vertical).
  • The second number is the Y-coordinate, which represents height. It’s less important for navigating horizontally.

② Cardinal Direction 【Importance: ★】

  • This shows which cardinal direction you're facing.
    • east → Direction of sunrise
    • west → Direction of sunset
    • south
    • north
  • In Minecraft, it’s often easier to navigate using X and Z coordinates than compass directions.

③ Direction Based on X/Z Coordinates 【Importance: ★★★★】

  • Shows which direction increases or decreases the X/Z values. It's a quick way to figure out which way you need to go.
    • positive X → X increases (East)
    • negative X → X decreases (West)
    • positive Z → Z increases (South)
    • negative Z → Z decreases (North)
  • Diagonal directions (e.g. northeast or southwest) may still appear as "positive X", etc.
  • If you're facing directly at a block, you’ll see an exact label like “pure positive X”.

④ Direction in Degrees 【Importance: ★★★】

  • Directions are also shown as angles between -180° and +180°.
  • Once you're used to it, you can adjust your facing direction by looking at the number.
  • On the map page, click the "Tools" button and enable "Mouse overlay display: Compass" to show a compass.
