Understanding Coordinates and Direction

When you press F3 in Minecraft, two key pieces of information are the coordinates and facing direction.

Example of coordinate and direction display (XYZ and positive X) shown using the F3 key

Coordinates (XYZ: ? / ? / ?)

  • Shows your current location in the world.
  • You’ll usually move based on X (horizontal) and Z (depth) values.
  • Y indicates your vertical height.

Direction (third line below: Toward ? ?)

  • Shows the direction your character is facing.
  • Examples:
    • positive X → Facing in the increasing X direction (East)
    • negative X → Decreasing X direction (West)
    • positive Z → Increasing Z direction (South)
    • negative Z → Decreasing Z direction (North)
  • Diagonal directions (e.g., Northeast, Southeast) are also labeled using the closest axis direction (e.g., "positive X" for East).
  • If you face a block head-on, you’ll see the exact direction like "pure East (positive X)".

Other Information

  • The numbers in parentheses after "positive X", etc., indicate the facing angle in degrees from -180 to +180:
    • North (-180), East (-90), South (0), West (90)
    • Facing exactly 0/90/180 helps you align precisely, but it’s often quicker to use block orientation.
  • The next line (Client Light) shows brightness levels from 0 to 15. Mobs spawn at level 7 or below.
    • The values in parentheses show light from the sky and from blocks, respectively.
  • The text at the very bottom of the screen indicates the current biome.
